Hi! I'm Adithya Vellal, and I'm a student at Duke studying Computer Science and Math.

I currently serve as one of Duke Undergraduate Machine Learning's co-presidents and help organize events such as the Duke Datathon, Machine Learning Day, and the MLBytes Speaker Series.

In the past, I've worked on numerous research and engineering projects at the intersection of machine learning and healthcare. I spent my past two summers interning at PathAI, where I developed deep learning algorithms for medical image alignment and investigated ways to speed up model training
and inference workflows for pathology image analysis.

I was also a researcher at Duke's Carl E. Ravin Imaging Labs, where I helped design general adversarial networks (GANs) to normalize breast MRIs produced by different types of MRI scanners. The paper detailing our work can be found here. Prior to that, I worked at Harvard Medical School, where I engineered a deep learning pipeline to quantitatively analyze digital biopsy images of benign breast disease patients. The papers resulting from this project can be found here, here, and here. The code is here.

Some other areas of interest include probability & decision-making, startups, and product strategy.
